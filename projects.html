1. ZH-CN to US-EN Speech to text translation

Through using Google Translate on my phone, I realized a large discrepancy between translating Chinese to English. In addition, there would sometimes be transcription errors from Chinese speech to Chinese text. So, my team and I tackled this problem by re-creating a Chinese to English pipeline with cutting edge transformer models for ASR, and neural networks for translation.

Tools used: ASR, PyTorch, Huggingface, Transformers, OpenNMT, Wav2Vec 2.0

2. Tweet Sentiment Analysis

After scraping the data and annotating 10,000+ tweets, we tried three approaches:

Supervised Learning:
  • Linear models: Logistic Regression
  • Non-linear Models: Naive Bayes, SVM, Decision Trees
  • Ensembles: Random Forest, LGBM, CatBoost
    • Unsupervised Learning:
  • K-means
  • SVD + Topic Modelling
  • Genism
    • Deep Learning Models:
  • Baseline: RNN
  • More complex sequence models: GRU, LSTM (bidirectional)
  • Integrated GloVe Embedding to improve performance
    • After hyperparamter tuning, our model had the least overfitting/underfitting, and also had the highest validation score of 78.78%

    Data Visualization

    Our dataset is trained on tweets from the last week of December 2020, and we noticed that the tweets contained many headlines from news outlets. So, we decided to label those as "Neutral". As shown in the graph, we had a large imbalanced dataset to deal with, since there were many auto-generated headline tweets, and not as many negative and positive tweets from real users.
    Being able to explain data visually is very important to us, since explaining data to other team members and stakeholders is an important part of being a good Data Scientist.
